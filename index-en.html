Featured Case

Questopia

Gamified spaced repetition for Middle School Portuguese

Status: Web MVP in testing Role: Founder & PM (solo)

The Problem

Three pain points around Middle School Portuguese:

Students

The "I studied but forgot everything" feeling in reading comprehension, grammar, and literature. Cramming before exams instead of distributed review.

Teachers

Overwhelmed by repetitive grading with little topic-level visibility. No consolidated data on what was actually learned.

Schools / Families

No objective data on Portuguese study happening outside the classroom.

Discovery

Research with students

  • Mapped study routines, attention competition, and ideal session formats
  • Insight: any solution requiring too many access steps tends to be forgotten.

Research with teachers

  • Lesson planning, grading workflows, and difficulties maintaining consistent reviews
  • Insight: the product must assist, not replace the teacher. Time saved on grading is a tangible value proposition.
  • Quantitative survey with 8-10 teachers being planned to measure actual time spent grading.

Design Decisions

Clear scope: Portuguese, grades 6-9

Tracks for reading comprehension, grammar, and literature aligned to Brazil's BNCC curriculum, instead of trying to cover every subject.

7-15 minute sessions

Designed to fit between classes and other activities. Spaced repetition algorithm with increasing intervals on correct answers, reset on errors.

Three distinct promises

Student: "you won't forget what you learned in class". Teacher: "save time and see where your class struggles". School/family: visibility into real study.

Gamification for habit

XP, daily goals, streaks, and class rankings to sustain engagement across short review sessions.

Web MVP Current phase

Functional prototype at questopia.com.br, built with AI/no-code (Lovable). Portuguese question catalog organized in tracks by grade and topic (reading comprehension, grammar, literature).

Next steps

  • Recruit 8-10 Portuguese teachers (~40-50 students) for a 2-3 week pilot
  • Track: % of students who log in and complete sessions, average sessions per student, accuracy rate changes
  • Collect teacher feedback on time saved and report clarity

Data-driven decision

With pilot data, decide whether the next iteration is UX improvements and web reminders, or an experiment on a lower-friction channel — if access remains the main bottleneck.
