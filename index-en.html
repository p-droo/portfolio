How I Work

My work always starts with diagnosis: understanding the client's context, mapping learner needs and identifying the gaps between where they are and where they need to be. I work with domestic and international clients in Portuguese, English, Spanish and German, adapting educational solutions to different cultural and organizational contexts.

Own EdTech Platform

Questopia

English teaching (EFL) platform based on spaced repetition, gamification and AI

Status: MVP in iteration Role: Founder & Learning Designer Research: Master's project in Design — UFPE

The Educational Challenge

Teaching English as a foreign language in Brazil faces a structural retention problem. Students study vocabulary and grammar, are assessed at a single point in time and move on — with no structured review. Cognitive science shows that spaced repetition is one of the most effective methods for long-term retention, but existing tools (such as Anki) require discipline and autonomy that most learners cannot sustain on their own.

EFL Student

Low retention of vocabulary and structures. Multiple activities competing for study time. Abandons solutions that require significant setup effort or autonomous discipline.

Teacher / Language School

Time spent on recurring review activities. No data on students' error patterns. Needs tools that complement (not replace) their pedagogical practice.

Diagnosis and Research

  • Qualitative research with English students to map study routines, barriers and openness to review tools
  • Key insight: solutions that compete with free time get abandoned — review must fit into the gaps between existing activities, preferably through channels the student already uses
  • Interviews with English teachers about workflow and operational pain points
  • Key insight: the tool must generate value for the teacher (error data, time savings) so they adopt it and recommend it to students

Educational Design Decisions

Short review sessions

Based on microlearning principles and spaced repetition. 5–10 minute sessions that fit into daily routines without competing with other activities.

Assist the teacher, not replace them

A decision driven by research. The teacher is the adoption channel — if they perceive a threat to their role, they won't recommend the tool.

Focus on EFL for Portuguese speakers

Strategic pivot: an audience with real urgency (job market, travel, certifications), without compliance barriers or parental mediation.

Gamification + AI for personalization

Progression mechanics and immediate feedback for engagement. AI to adapt level and content to each learner's individual performance.

Iterations

Iteration 1 — Web MVP (Middle School)

First functional prototype at questopia.com.br, aimed at students aged 10–14. Initial tests revealed low adoption: the audience lacked sufficient urgency to sustain engagement — spaced review competed with more attractive activities without a clear motivator. Additionally, the audience presented compliance and parental consent barriers for academic research.

Iteration 2 — Pivot to EFL (current phase)

Strategic redirection toward teaching English as a foreign language for Brazilian Portuguese speakers. The new audience is more accessible (young adults and adults), has real urgency (job market, certifications, exams) and doesn't face parental consent issues — making it viable as a research project for the Master's in Design at UFPE. The platform is live at english.questopia.com.br, currently testing engagement and seeking active users to validate or invalidate the retention and adoption theses.

Social Impact Educational Program

Pro Criança — Educational Program Design

Social impact · 100+ students · 2019

Challenge: An existing educational program with low engagement and learning outcomes below expectations. It lacked a structured journey to guide participants from first contact to practical application.

Solution: I redesigned the learning journey focused on key value moments — clear initial onboarding, gradual progression and continuous feedback. Applied learning psychology to reduce cognitive load and increase retention, with a curriculum focused on learner autonomy.

Result: Significant increase in engagement and completion, impacting 100+ students. The same principles of good educational design — reducing friction, creating early value, supporting the journey — apply to formal, corporate and digital education alike.
