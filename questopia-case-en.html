Overview

Questopia is a gamified spaced-repetition platform for Middle School Portuguese (grades 6-9).

It helps students retain reading comprehension, grammar, and literature through short review sessions, while giving teachers clear data on common errors and class progress.

Context & Problem

After more than 15 years teaching Portuguese, I kept seeing the same pattern: students quickly forgot content they had "already seen", and teachers spent hours grading practice activities without a consolidated view of what had actually been learned.

In Brazilian Middle School, the national curriculum (BNCC) requires students to make measurable progress in reading, writing, and language analysis across grades 6-9. In practice, review often happens in crammed sessions right before exams instead of being distributed over time, which leads to:

Students Feeling "I studied this and forgot everything" in reading, grammar rules, and literature.
Teachers Overloaded with repetitive grading and little insight by topic.
Schools and families Lacking objective data about Portuguese study outside the classroom.

Users and Value Proposition

Curriculum focus

Portuguese content organized for grades 6-9:

  • Reading comprehension (poems, chronicles, informational texts, etc.)
  • Grammar (parts of speech, morphology, syntax, punctuation)
  • Literature (works, genres, reading context)

Three stakeholders

Students (grades 6-9) "Don't forget what you already learned in class." Short, game-like review sessions that help them remember what they have already covered.
Portuguese teachers "Save time and see exactly where your class is struggling." Automated practice with item-level data by topic (e.g., pronouns, concord, reading poetry) instead of piles of worksheets.
Schools / families "Have minimum visibility into real study, not just homework handed in." Simple progress indicators tied to core Portuguese skills.

Student experience

  • 7-15 minute review sessions designed to fit between classes and homework.
  • Spaced-repetition engine that decides when each question returns: intervals increase with correct answers, reset with wrong answers so weaker topics appear more often.
  • Gamification to sustain engagement: XP for each question, daily goals, streaks, and a class leaderboard.

Teacher experience

Dashboard with:

  • Accuracy by student and by topic (e.g., pronouns, verb tenses, reading poetry).
  • History of attempts so teachers can quickly see what needs to be revisited in class.

My Role & Process

I act as Founder and Product Manager for Questopia, responsible for research, product definition, and coordinating development using AI/no-code tools.

Discovery

  • Conducted exploratory interviews with Middle School students to understand their study routines, attention competition (social media, games, etc.), and tolerance for extra work.
  • Interviewed Portuguese teachers to map their planning and grading workflow, and how they currently handle review.

Key insights: any solution that feels like "another big platform to log into" is easily forgotten by students; teachers do not want to be replaced, they want support and actionable data; "saving grading time" and "seeing recurring errors by topic" are strong value propositions that need to be backed by real numbers.

Product decisions

  • Narrowed the scope from "all subjects" to Portuguese only, grades 6-9, aligned with BNCC topics so teachers immediately recognize where it fits in their syllabus.
  • Designed short sessions (7-15 minutes) based on student feedback on what is realistically doable between other tasks.
  • Framed three distinct promises:
    • For students: "don't forget what you already learned in class."
    • For teachers: "save time and see exactly where your class is struggling."
    • For schools/families: "have minimum visibility into real study, not just homework handed in."

MVP Web

Built a functional web MVP at questopia.com.br using Lovable (AI-assisted / no-code) based on flows and information architecture I defined.

  • Loaded an initial set of Portuguese questions structured in tracks by grade and topic (reading, grammar, literature).
  • Ran early tests with a small group of students and teachers in my network to observe:
    • How many students actually signed up and completed at least one session.
    • Where they dropped off in the flow.
    • Which topics generated more errors.

Early Learnings

1. A clear scope makes everything easier

Focusing on Middle School Portuguese made it much easier to explain the product and recruit teachers for pilots.

2. Access friction is a critical hypothesis

Adoption still suffers from access friction: students understand the value when explained, but many forget to return on their own.

3. Teachers want review + data

Teachers react positively to the idea of "ongoing review + topic-level data", and I am now preparing a quick survey with 8-10 teachers to quantify current grading time and the potential savings.

What's Next

Before considering any new channel, my priority is to make the focused web MVP work for Middle School Portuguese:

  • Recruit 8-10 Portuguese teachers and aim for ~5 students each (~40-50 students) to run a 2-3 week pilot using the current web platform.
  • Track:
    • % of invited students who log in and complete at least one session.
    • Average number of sessions per student.
    • Change in accuracy on a few selected topics over the pilot period.
    • Teacher feedback on time saved (perceived) and clarity of reports.
  • With those data points, update the product and decide — based on evidence — whether the next iteration is:
    • Further UX and reminder improvements on web, or
    • A complementary experiment in a lower-friction channel (e.g., messaging), if access remains the main bottleneck.
