Executive Summary

Questopia is a gamified assessment web platform for 10â€“14 year old students (Middle School). The project was created to solve a dual bottleneck: teacher workload in grading assessments and low student engagement with traditional methods.

I led the product from hypothesis-driven discovery to the construction and iteration of an MVP. The current goal is to validate an estimated saving of 10 hours per week per teacher and increase student retention through short, dynamic sessions.

The Problem & Opportunity

The Middle School Gap

The EdTech market tends to focus on literacy or college prep. The "middle ground" (ages 12-14) suffers from a lack of innovation. We identified that students in this age group have low persistence in structured activities, and teachers are drowning in manual grading.

The Opportunity: Create a product that simultaneously delivers continuous engagement for teenagers (via gamification and spaced repetition) and data intelligence for educators/parents.

Discovery & Research

To reduce uncertainty before writing a single line of code, I executed a discovery process based on 4 axes of hypotheses:

1. Behavior

Mobile devices and social networks compete aggressively for student attention.

2. Teacher Training

Teachers feel unprepared to handle social-emotional demands.

3. Autonomy

External pressures (parents/school) limit pedagogical innovation in the classroom.

4. School Climate

Operational overload prevents individualized monitoring.

Methodology: I applied structured questionnaires segmented by years of experience. The result validated the latent pain: experienced teachers (>5 years) feel the administrative burden more than the pedagogical one.

Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)

We mapped the "jobs" that each stakeholder is trying to accomplish:

Teacher "I want to automate assessment (functional) so I can focus on teaching better classes and feel effective (emotional)."
Student "I want to improve my grades (functional) without looking like I'm studying all the time and losing my free time (social)."
Parents "I want visibility into my child's progress (functional) to reduce my anxiety about their future (emotional)."

The Main Pivot (Learning)

Initially, we focused on "Assessment". We discovered that this generates resistance. We pivoted the communication to "Self-Knowledge" and "Learning Style". Result: Students began to see value for themselves, not just for the school, which drastically increased organic engagement.
